Summary

I am a researcher at the intersection of biomedical science and artificial intelligence. My work focuses on developing robust, clinically meaningful models for diagnosis, prognosis and treatment stratification by combining multimodal medical data such as histopathology, radiology, molecular and clinical data. I have authored over 40 peer‑reviewed publications, achieving an H‑index of 23 and accumulating more than 3,200 citations. My ultimate goal is to advance translational medical AI that improves patient care.

Research Interests

My research focuses on multimodal medical AI, where heterogeneous data sources (histopathology, radiology, molecular and clinical data) are integrated to build models that are robust, generalizable and clinically relevant. I am particularly interested in vision–language models, contrastive learning, zero‑shot generalization and spatial transcriptomics for histopathology. I am also actively exploring large language models and agentic workflows for healthcare applications.

Highlighted Projects

  • Vision–Language Embeddings for Histopathology: Leveraging vision–language models to enable zero‑shot classification of histopathology images across diverse cancer types.
  • Generalized Deep Learning for Histopathology: Developing contrastive learning frameworks to improve generalization and robustness of CNNs and transformers in medical image analysis.
  • Spatial Transcriptomics & Prognostics: Integrating spatial transcriptomics with histopathology images to predict gene expression and patient outcomes in breast cancer.
  • DeepCervix Framework: A hybrid deep learning method for automated classification of cervical cells using feature‑fusion techniques.
